CHIAREZZA SCIENTIFICA:
---------------------

13. **SECTION 3.4 - STATISTICAL ANALYSIS**
    Issue: "24 pairwise comparisons" è ora spiegato MA ancora ambiguo
    Current explanation: "derived from testing 3 model backends across 8 
                         distinct encodings"
    Problem: Non è chiaro se sono:
             - 3 backends × 8 encodings = 24 (ma non sono pairwise!)
             - C(3,2) × 8 = 3 × 8 = 24 pairwise backend comparisons
             - 3 × C(8,2) = 3 × 28 = 84 pairwise encoding comparisons
    
    Fix: Specificare ESATTAMENTE:
    "To control the Family-Wise Error Rate (FWER) when comparing backends 
    pairwise across encodings, we applied Bonferroni correction for 24 
    comparisons: C(3,2) = 3 pairwise backend comparisons (XGB vs Cat, 
    XGB vs DT, Cat vs DT) × 8 encodings = 24 tests, yielding 
    α = 0.05/24 = 0.0021."

14. **SECTION 4.2 - "BACKEND ROBUSTNESS" PARAGRAFO**
    Issue: Si dice "completely outperforming" ma poi "both models confirmed 
           robust cross-assay resilience" → suona contradditorio
    Current: "gradient boosting frameworks successfully captured generalized 
             biological patterns, completely outperforming the Decision Tree 
             baseline"
    Better: "gradient boosting frameworks successfully captured generalized 
            biological patterns, substantially outperforming the Decision Tree 
            baseline (which collapsed to near-zero predictive power)"

15. **SECTION 5 - SHAP SAMPLE SIZE**
    Issue: "10,000 GUIDEseq examples" suona arbitrario
    Current: "SHAP values were aggregated over a representative sample of 
             10,000 GUIDEseq test examples."
    Better: "To ensure computational tractability while maintaining statistical 
            representativeness, we computed SHAP values on a stratified random 
            sample of 10,000 examples from the GUIDEseq test set."

Per SECTION 6 (Conclusion) - AGGIUNGERE:
-----------------------------------------

Dopo l'attuale testo, prima di "The choice of sequence encoding...", inserire:

\textbf{Limitations and Future Work:}
"While our study demonstrates robust cross-assay generalization, several 
limitations should be acknowledged. First, our evaluation focuses exclusively 
on SpCas9, and transferability to other Cas variants (e.g., Cas12a, Cas13) 
remains to be validated. Second, we evaluate only two assay types; inclusion 
of additional experimental platforms (e.g., CIRCLE-seq, SITE-seq) would 
provide further validation of the encoding strategies. Finally, our models 
rely solely on sequence features; integration of epigenetic information 
(e.g., chromatin accessibility, DNA methylation) may improve in vivo 
prediction accuracy. These directions represent promising avenues for 
extending this framework toward clinical translation."


Per TABLE 2 Caption - MIGLIORARE:
---------------------------------

Da:
"Top GUIDEseq Performance (OOF). Macro-average of metrics calculated 
independently for each of the 110 hold-out sgRNAs."

A:
"Best-performing configurations per backend on GUIDEseq test set. Metrics 
represent macro-averages calculated independently for each of the 87 test 
sgRNAs using the OOF strategy. For each backend, we report the top-2 
encodings by performance."


Per SECTION 3.4 (Statistical Analysis) - CHIARIRE:
--------------------------------------------------

Da:
"To control the Family-Wise Error Rate (FWER), all reported p-values were 
adjusted using the Bonferroni correction (24 pairwise comparisons, derived 
from testing 3 model backends across 8 distinct encodings, α = 0.05/24 = 0.0021)."

A:
"To control the Family-Wise Error Rate (FWER), all reported p-values were 
adjusted using the Bonferroni correction. For backend comparisons, we 
performed C(3,2) = 3 pairwise tests (XGBoost vs CatBoost, XGBoost vs 
Decision Tree, CatBoost vs Decision Tree) across 8 encodings, yielding 
24 total comparisons (α = 0.05/24 = 0.0021)."
